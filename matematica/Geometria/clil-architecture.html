1. The Ellipse in Urban Spaces

When we walk through historic Italian cities, we often admire grand open spaces called piazzas (squares). Despite the name, the most famous and majestic ones are actually rarely square-shaped! Instead, architects have often used a specific geometric shape we studied in the Conics lesson: the Ellipse.

Why use an ellipse? Unlike a circle which draws attention only to a single point (the center), an ellipse features two focal points (foci). This creates a dynamic sense of movement and "embraces" the crowd, making huge spaces feel both monumental and welcoming.

  • St. Peter's Square (Rome): Designed by Gian Lorenzo Bernini, the immense colonnade is a perfect mathematical ellipse. Bernini wanted the colonnade to symbolize the "maternal arms of the Church" embracing the faithful. If you stand exactly on one of the two focal points marked on the ground, the four rows of columns miraculously perfectly align into a single row!
  • Piazza del Plebiscito (Naples): This vast, breathtaking space is defined by an elegant elliptical sweep. The elliptical colonnade draws the eyes naturally toward the massive dome of the church, acting as a stunning theatrical stage for the city.

2. The Golden Ratio (La Sezione Aurea)

What makes a building look "naturally" beautiful? Many artists and architects have relied on a specific mathematical proportion known as the Golden Ratio (or Divine Proportion). Represented by the Greek letter Phi (\( \Phi \)), its value is approximately 1.618.

📐 The Mathematical Definition
The Golden Ratio occurs when you divide a line into two parts: the longer part (\(a\)) and the shorter part (\(b\)), such that the ratio of the whole line (\(a + b\)) to the longer part (\(a\)) is exactly equal to the ratio of the longer part (\(a\)) to the shorter part (\(b\))!
Equation: \( \frac{a+b}{a} = \frac{a}{b} = \Phi \approx 1.618 \)

This divine proportion is not just an abstract number; it appears everywhere in nature (like the spiral of a nautilus shell) and has been intentionally applied in human history:

  • The Parthenon (Greece): Ancient Greeks used Golden Rectangles to establish the perfect width and height of the majestic facade, achieving total aesthetic harmony.
  • Renaissance Art: Geniuses like Leonardo da Vinci and architects like Le Corbusier in modern times explicitly used \(\Phi\) to dictate the heights of windows, doors, and the physical proportions of their buildings.

🇬🇧 CLIL Vocabulary & Glossary

  • Square / Piazza: An open public area in a city or town.
  • Focal point (Focus): A central point of attention or a geometric point used to define a conic section.
  • Golden Ratio (Divine Proportion): A mathematical ratio (1.618) representing perfect harmonious proportions.
  • Facade: The principal front or face of a building.
  • Colonnade: A sequence of columns joined by an entablature.
  • Embrace: To hold someone closely in one's arms (used metaphorically for Bernini's colonnade).
