CLIL: Carl Friedrich Gauss

The "Prince of Mathematicians" who outsmarted his teacher in primary school.

Who was Gauss?

Carl Friedrich Gauss (1777–1855) was a German mathematician and physicist. He is often called the "Princeps Mathematicorum" (Latin for "Prince of Mathematicians") because of his enormous contributions to almost every field of science, from geometry and number theory to astronomy and electromagnetism.

Gauss was a true child prodigy. It is said that at three years old, he corrected an error in his father's bookkeeping!

The "1 to 100" Trick

Story goes that when Gauss was only 7 or 10 years old, his teacher gave the class a long task to keep them quiet: "Sum all the numbers from 1 to 100!"

While the other students were sweating over their slates, young Gauss placed his down in seconds. How? He realized that by pairing the numbers, he always got the same result:

  • \(1 + 100 = 101\)
  • \(2 + 99 = 101\)
  • \(3 + 98 = 101\)
  • ...and so on!

Since there are 100 numbers, there are 50 pairs of 101. So, the sum is $50 \times 101 = 5050$. Brilliant!

Interactive: The Secret Formula

This trick works for any number \(n\). If you want to sum all numbers from 1 to \(n\), the formula is: \[\sum_{i=1}^n i = \frac{n(n + 1)}{2}\]

Math Memes: Gauss vs Chuck Norris

Mathematicians love to joke about Gauss's legendary intellect. Here are some of the best "Gauss facts" (like Chuck Norris facts):

"Gauss doesn't need to prove his theorems. They define themselves as true in his presence."
"When Gauss counts to 10, he counts to infinity... twice."
"Gauss once calculated the value of $\pi$ in his head. $\pi$ just turned into 3 out of respect."
"Gauss's primary school teacher is still trying to get him to show his working."
"Chuck Norris can divide by zero. Without getting an error."
"Chuck Norris doesn't do math. He just stares at the equation until it solves itself out of fear."
"Chuck Norris counted to infinity. Exactly 101 times."
